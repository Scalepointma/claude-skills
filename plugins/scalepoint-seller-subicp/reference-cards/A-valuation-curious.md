# Sub-ICP A · Valuation-Curious

> **Internal label.** Never quote "Valuation-Curious" back to the seller.

## Essence

Just looking. Not selling. Yet. Maybe ever.

Owner exploring what their business is worth, with no current commitment to selling and no active prep happening anywhere in their life.

## Telltale signs

- Opens with "what's it worth?" before any question about selling
- Hedge phrases: "just curious," "thinking ahead," "maybe never"
- No accountant, lawyer, or advisor involved in the conversation yet
- Came in through valuation calculator or exit readiness quiz, not through a buyer-side touchpoint
- Can't name a triggering event or a target year

## Easy to confuse with

**Sub-ICP B (Pre-Seller).** Distinguishing question: *"In the last 90 days, have you talked to an accountant, lawyer, or family member about selling?"* B has. A hasn't.

## First-meeting move

Do NOT pitch the Engagement Letter. Do NOT introduce to any buyer.

- Drop into long-cycle nurture
- Send the valuation report
- Offer the Exit Readiness Quiz
- Add to the quarterly Deal Pulse cohort (Alina-authored, BASAB-content-fed)
- Re-touch every 6–12 months until something shifts

- A starts talking to an accountant about cleanup → B
- A's life changes (health, divorce, partner exit) → E
