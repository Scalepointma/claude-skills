# Sub-ICP D · Sophisticated FSBO

> **Internal label.** OK to use externally if useful — "FSBO" is industry-standard and the seller likely self-identifies that way.

## Essence

$2–10M deal. Not paying a broker $500K to do what I can manage.

Decided seller of a $2–10M business with clean accountant-prepared financials, a management team in place, who has consciously chosen FSBO over commission-based representation. Highest LTV per deal cohort — match these meetings carefully.

## Telltale signs

- Accountant-prepared statements, possibly a recasting workbook ready
- Asks about working capital adjustments, escrow holdback, earn-out structures, rep & warranty
- Has interviewed brokers and decided commission math doesn't work at their deal size
- Can describe how the business runs without them (team, ops, recurring revenue)
- Language: "strategic exit," "monetize," "post-close transition"

## Easy to confuse with

- **C (Committed Main Street).** Distinguishing question: *"Do you have accountant-prepared statements (Notice-to-Reader, Review, or Audited)?"* D yes; C usually no. Also: *"If you stopped working tomorrow, would the business still run?"* D yes (team in place); C no (owner is the business).
- **G (Broker-Represented).** Distinguishing question: *"Are you currently under engagement with another broker?"* G yes; D no by choice.

## Match Matrix verdicts for row D

| Buyer cohort | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Strategic Acquirer | **Strong** |
| Searcher (ETA) | Possible |
| Owner-Operator | Weak |
| LP / Passive | **Strong** |
| Family Office | **Strong** |
| Holdco / Roll-up | **Strong** |
| Searcher-Broker | Possible |

**Four Strong matches: Strategic, LP, Family Office, Holdco.** Funds and Strategic Acquirers are the natural match — they have the capital, the underwriting muscle for $2–10M deals, and they want clean financials. Owner-Operator is Weak — they can't afford $2–10M, and the business is too sophisticated for a buy-a-job buyer. Searcher and Searcher-Broker are Possible — depends on their fund size and search thesis.

## First-meeting move

Slow down. D respects expert presentation.

- Lead with a Deal Room demo and a real CIM offer
- Engagement Letter belongs in the second or third meeting, after they've seen the work product
- Be prepared to negotiate against their FSBO posture — they may want a hybrid (consulting fee + smaller success fee) instead of standard EL
- Pitch the appropriate fee band ($5M–<$10M at 3% with $20K retainer, or $10M+ at 3%/5% with $25K retainer)

## What changes D → something else

- D decides to drop their FSBO posture and engage a broker fully → still D (the categorization is about the seller profile, not the engagement type)
- D engages another broker before us → G
- D loses operational team and starts running the business themselves again → re-evaluate (rare)
