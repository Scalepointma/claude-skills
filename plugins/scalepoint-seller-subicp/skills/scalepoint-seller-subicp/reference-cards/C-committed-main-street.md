# Sub-ICP C · Committed Main Street Seller

> **Internal label.** "Committed Main Street Seller" replaces earlier internal shorthand. Never use the legacy shorthand externally.

## Essence

I want out, this year if I can. The business runs on me and I'm tired.

Decided seller, $300K–$1M revenue, business runs on them personally, informal books, ready to sell this year if a buyer materializes. The volume cohort — this is most of ScalePoint's deal flow.

## Telltale signs

- Has an asking price in mind; says "I'd take X"
- Has been thinking about selling for years, finally decided this quarter
- Files T4s, not GAAP financials; bookkeeping is paper, spreadsheets, or basic QuickBooks
- Cites a concrete reason (retirement, health, "kids don't want it," "I'm tired")
- Wants buyer meetings; doesn't care about a polished CIM

## Easy to confuse with

- **D (Sophisticated FSBO).** Distinguishing question: *"If you stopped working tomorrow, would the business still run?"* C says "no, I am the business." D says "yes, my team can hold it for a while." D also has accountant-prepared statements; C usually doesn't.
- **B (Pre-Seller).** Distinguishing question: *"If a qualified buyer walked in tomorrow with a fair offer, would you take it?"* C says "yes if the number is right." B says "I'd want to be prepared first."

**Four Strong matches: Strategic, Searcher, Owner-Operator, Searcher-Broker.** Owner-Operator buyers especially want exactly what C is selling. Funds (LP, Family Office) are weak — deal too small, EBITDA underwriting harder on informal books. Holdco is Possible — depends on whether C fits their roll-up thesis.

## First-meeting move

Push toward Engagement Letter inside the first or second meeting. C rewards speed and decisiveness.

- Don't over-package; a tight teaser and direct buyer intros beat a 40-page CIM
- Pitch the appropriate fee band (likely Sub-$1M at 8% with $2,500 cancellation, or $1M–<$5M at 5% with $15K retainer)
- Make clear what handover support looks like (most C sellers need 3–6 months because they ARE the business)

## What changes C → something else

- C's books get cleaned by an accountant and they cross $2M revenue → re-evaluate as D
- Life event tightens timeline drastically → E
- C cools off and decides "maybe next year" → back to B
