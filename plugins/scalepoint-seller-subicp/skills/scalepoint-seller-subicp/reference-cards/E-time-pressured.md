# Sub-ICP E · Time-Pressured Seller

> **Internal label.** "Time-Pressured Seller" replaces "Forced Seller." Never use "forced" externally — it's both internally insensitive and externally damaging if the seller hears it.

## Essence

I have to sell because life is happening. Speed matters more than max price.

Seller of any size whose timeline is being driven by life events rather than market conditions.

## Telltale signs

- Names a deadline unprompted ("I need to close by X")
- Mentions the triggering event in the first 10 minutes
- More flexible on price than on timeline; will pay expedite fees
- Books may be clean or chaotic — their time is the variable, not their preparation
- May be one partner pushing the other; may be solo

## Easy to confuse with

- **C and D.** The difference is direction: C and D are driving the timeline; E is being driven by it. Distinguishing question: *"If this didn't close in 6 months, what would happen?"* C says "I'd be disappointed." E says "I can't let that happen."

**Four Strong matches: Strategic, Searcher, Owner-Operator, Searcher-Broker.** Speed-to-close beats match precision. LP is Weak — their underwriting cycle can't match the timeline. Family Office and Holdco are Possible if they happen to have capital ready and a thesis fit, but not first-tier outreach.

## First-meeting move

Move fast and be honest about what's possible in their window.

- Engagement Letter same week if circumstances warrant
- Discretion clauses matter more than usual — many E sellers don't want staff, customers, or family to know yet
- Push the appropriate fee band, possibly with an expedite premium negotiated in
- Pre-stage the Vault Room (or Preview at minimum) so we can move within hours of EL signing
- Tell them honestly which buyer cohorts can match their timeline — set expectations now, not after week 4

## What changes E → something else

- The triggering event resolves (health stabilizes, partner reconciles) → re-evaluate as C, D, or back to B
- E's timeline extends past 12 months → re-evaluate as C or D
- E discovers their financials are too thin to attract real buyers → still E, but coaching may be needed to get to listable quality
