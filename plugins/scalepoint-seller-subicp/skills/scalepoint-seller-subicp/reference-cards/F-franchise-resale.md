# Sub-ICP F · Franchise Resale Seller

> **Internal label.** "Franchise Resale" is industry-standard and OK to use externally.

## Essence

I'm selling a franchise unit and the franchisor is in the way.

Seller of one or more franchise units within an established brand, where the franchisor holds approval rights and shapes the deal. F overrides every other category — a Time-Pressured franchise reseller is still F, not E.

## Telltale signs

- Names the brand within the first 5 minutes (Tim Hortons, Subway, Anytime Fitness, Mr. Lube)
- References "the franchisor," "corporate," "approval process," "transfer fee"
- Knows the brand's typical multiple, often quoted by franchise development
- Lease and supply agreements tied to the franchisor
- May have multiple units in different stages of performance

## Easy to confuse with

- **C and D depending on unit size.** The distinguishing factor is the franchisor's veto. Distinguishing question: *"Does the brand need to approve any buyer?"* If yes, F regardless of other variables.

## Match Matrix verdicts for row F

| Buyer cohort | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Strategic Acquirer | **Strong** (within same brand) |
| Searcher (ETA) | **Strong** |
| Owner-Operator | Possible |
| LP / Passive | Weak |
| Family Office | Weak |
| Holdco / Roll-up | Possible |
| Searcher-Broker | **Strong** |

**Three Strong matches: Strategic (within same brand especially), Searcher with franchise experience, Searcher-Broker.** Strategic Acquirers within the same brand close fastest — they're often already approved. Searcher and Searcher-Broker who know the franchise model can navigate the franchisor approval process. Owner-Operator is Possible if they're willing to be brand-approved. Funds are Weak — vertical fit and brand approval usually fail.

## First-meeting move

Map the franchisor's approval timeline before anything else.

- Engagement Letter is fine, but pre-qualify any buyer for franchisor approval before formal intro
- Build the franchisor relationship as a side project — future F sellers will come through the brand
- Confirm Right of First Refusal (ROFR) status: if the franchisor or another franchisee has ROFR, that shapes the entire outreach strategy
- Capture the transfer fee — it's a deduction from the seller's net and from any structuring conversation

## What changes F → something else

- The franchise agreement ends or franchisor relationship terminates and seller is selling assets independently → re-evaluate as C, D, or asset sale
- Seller is selling all units AND the brand rights (rare) → consult Jodi; not standard F
